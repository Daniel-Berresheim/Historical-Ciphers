Historical Ciphers


Once upon a time, cryptographical algorithms were designed by tampering the messages in an obscure way which the creater could not decrypt himself, many of those did not even introduce keys and relied purely on the secrecy of the algorithm.

However, it is a misjudgment to think that the algorithm will not be leaked eventually. This effect increases even further with the growing power of modern computers. On the contrary, modern encryption schemes are mathematically proven to be secure under certain assumptions. Still, historical ciphers can be used to learn the basic concepts of cryptography and their weaknesses.

This website features many of the most common substitution ciphers of the old days. It is important to understand that these encryption methods should not be used in real world applications, they are only educational.
